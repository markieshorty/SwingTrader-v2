How Acme Trading works

This guide explains what the system does and shows your account's current settings inline, so it always reflects how your bot is actually configured. You're currently in {{ currentMode() }} mode.

The daily pipeline

Acme Trading runs as a chain of scheduled agents. Each does one job and hands off to the next. All times listed below are in ET, the market's timezone (times elsewhere in the app are shown in your local time).

  1. Watchlist (weekly, Sun 20:00) — screens the S&P 500 / Nasdaq 100 down to a focused list of candidates.
  2. Research (daily, 07:30) — scores every watchlist symbol 0–10 on the eight components in the next tab, using fresh pre-market data. An optional midday rescore (off by default) refreshes the same day's scores at 12:30.
  3. Report (daily, 08:30) — emails you a brief of the day's buy/watch/hold/avoid signals.
  4. Approval — if enabled, nothing trades until you approve. Approvals are on the Trades page. One approval covers the whole trading day, including any midday-rescored signals — you are never re-asked intraday.
  5. Execution (daily, from 09:20) — places orders for approved buy signals at Trading 212.
  6. Monitor (every 5 min, 09:30–16:00) — watches open positions and enforces stops, targets, trailing stops and exits.
  7. Risk (monthly) & Refinement (monthly) — re-evaluate your capital tier and fine-tune the component weights.
  8. Readiness (daily) — records how close each feature is to being trusted with more capital.

How a signal is scored

Every candidate stock gets a single conviction score from 0 to 10 — the higher the number, the stronger the case for buying it. That score is a weighted blend of eight separate checks. Each check scores the stock from 0 to 1, that score is multiplied by the check's weight (how much it counts), and the results are added up and scaled to 10.

The weights below are your account's current values and add up to 100%. They aren't fixed — the Refinement agent gradually shifts weight toward whichever checks have actually predicted your winning trades. Tap any check to see what it means in plain terms.

What the score decides

  • Score ≥ {{ weights()?.buyThreshold ?? '—' }} → Buy — strong enough to actually trade (subject to approval).
  • Score ≥ {{ weights()?.watchThreshold ?? '—' }} → Watch — interesting, kept on the radar but not traded.
  • Below that → Hold / Avoid — not acted on.

If a check can't get its data (say a news feed is down), it counts as a neutral 0.5 rather than dragging the score down — so a missing data point can never nudge a stock toward a Buy it didn't earn.

How positions are exited

Buying is only half the job — knowing when to sell is what protects the account. Once a trade is open the Monitor agent watches it continuously and closes it automatically when any of the following triggers. You never have to sell manually. All the numbers below are your current settings (change them on Settings → Risk profile). Each position locks its rules in at buy time — hold limits, probation and trailing-stop shape, like the stop and target — so changing a setting affects positions opened from then on, never ones already open.

Stop loss — caps the downside {{ pct(weights()?.stopLossPctDefault) }} below entry

A price at which the trade is sold automatically to stop a small loss becoming a big one. If you buy at £100 and the stop is {{ pct(weights()?.stopLossPctDefault) }}, the position sells if the price falls to roughly £{{ stopExample() }}. Every trade has one — it's the single most important risk control.

Profit target — takes the win ≈ 8–12% above entry

The price at which the trade is sold to bank a profit. It's set per-trade from the setup type and how strong the conviction was (stronger signals aim a little higher). If price hits the target, Monitor sells and locks in the gain.

Trailing stop — protects a winner arms at +{{ pct(risk()?.trailingActivationPct) }}, trails by {{ pct(risk()?.trailingDistancePct) }}

Once a trade is up by {{ pct(risk()?.trailingActivationPct) }}, a second stop switches on that follows the price upward but never moves back down. It sits {{ pct(risk()?.trailingDistancePct) }} below the highest price reached. So if a winner keeps climbing the stop climbs with it, and if the price then drops {{ pct(risk()?.trailingDistancePct) }} from its peak the trade sells — letting winners run while still banking most of the gain if they reverse.

Time exit — frees up stuck capital {{ risk()?.maxHoldDays ?? '—' }} days

If a position hasn't hit its target or stop after {{ risk()?.maxHoldDays ?? '—' }} days, it's closed anyway. Money tied up in a trade that's going nowhere can't fund a better opportunity — swing trades are meant to play out over days, not months.

Momentum health check — exits a broken thesis from day {{ risk()?.minHoldDays ?? '—' }}

After a minimum hold of {{ risk()?.minHoldDays ?? '—' }} days, Monitor re-checks whether the reasons it bought still hold. If the momentum that justified the trade has clearly broken down, it exits early rather than waiting for the full stop loss to trigger.

Earnings blackout — avoids a coin-flip {{ risk()?.earningsGateDays ?? '—' }} days

The bot won't open a new position within {{ risk()?.earningsGateDays ?? '—' }} days of a company's earnings announcement. Earnings are unpredictable, all-or-nothing events that can gap the price far past any stop loss overnight — not the kind of risk this strategy takes.

Two completely separate worlds

You're currently in {{ currentMode() }} mode. Demo trades practice money against Trading 212's demo environment; Live trades real money. They use different API credentials and are kept entirely separate — trades, reports, readiness, refinement and P&L for one mode never mix into the other.

  • Each mode needs its own Trading 212 key + secret pair (demo keys are rejected by the live endpoint and vice-versa).
  • Switching mode takes effect immediately and reloads the app so every figure reflects the new mode.
  • If you switch with positions still open in the current mode, you'll be warned first — the bot stops monitoring those positions until you switch back, so they'd sit without stop-loss protection.

Tip: use the Test button on Settings → API Keys. It calls Trading 212 and shows the account balance and whether it's practice or real money — the quickest way to confirm your keys are correct and not swapped.

Getting set up

The bot needs four integrations, all entered on Settings → API Keys:

  • Finnhub — quotes, company news and profiles.
  • Tiingo — historical price candles for indicators.
  • Claude — news-sentiment scoring and written narratives.
  • Trading 212 — a key + secret pair for whichever mode(s) you'll run.

The approval flow

Approval is currently {{ approvalOn() ? 'ON' : 'OFF' }}. When on, the daily Report lists the proposed buys and nothing executes until you approve on the Trades → Approvals tab. There's a daily window (the Report tells you the cutoff); no action means no trades that day. When off, approved-by-default buy signals execute automatically at the Execution window.

Approvals are per-day and per-mode. Same-day buys that get re-triggered after a position closes are handled automatically without needing re-approval.
