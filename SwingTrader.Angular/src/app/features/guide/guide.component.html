How SwingTrader works

This guide explains what the system does and shows your account's current settings inline, so it always reflects how your bot is actually configured. You're currently in {{ currentMode() }} mode.

The daily pipeline

SwingTrader runs as a chain of scheduled agents. Each does one job and hands off to the next. All times listed below are in ET, the market's timezone (times elsewhere in the app are shown in your local time).

  1. Watchlist (weekly, Sun 20:00) — screens the S&P 500 / Nasdaq 100 down to a focused list of candidates.
  2. Research (daily, 04:00) — scores every watchlist symbol 0–10 on the eight components in the next tab.
  3. Report (daily, 06:30) — emails you a brief of the day's buy/watch/hold/avoid signals.
  4. Approval — if enabled, nothing trades until you approve. Approvals are on the Trades page.
  5. Execution (daily, from 09:20) — places orders for approved buy signals at Trading 212.
  6. Monitor (every 5 min, 09:30–16:00) — watches open positions and enforces stops, targets, trailing stops and exits.
  7. Risk (monthly) & Refinement (monthly) — re-evaluate your capital tier and fine-tune the component weights.
  8. Readiness (daily) — records how close each feature is to being trusted with more capital.

How a signal is scored

Every candidate gets a single conviction score from 0 to 10. It's a weighted blend of eight independent components — each scored 0–1, multiplied by its weight, then summed and scaled to 10. The weights below are your account's current values (they sum to 100%). The Refinement agent nudges them over time based on which components actually predicted your winners.

Thresholds

When a component's data source is unavailable (e.g. a news fetch fails), it contributes a neutral 0.5 rather than dragging the score down. A missing data point never pushes a signal toward Buy.

Two completely separate worlds

You're currently in {{ currentMode() }} mode. Demo trades practice money against Trading 212's demo environment; Live trades real money. They use different API credentials and are kept entirely separate — trades, reports, readiness, refinement and P&L for one mode never mix into the other.

  • Each mode needs its own Trading 212 key + secret pair (demo keys are rejected by the live endpoint and vice-versa).
  • Switching mode takes effect immediately and reloads the app so every figure reflects the new mode.
  • If you switch with positions still open in the current mode, you'll be warned first — the bot stops monitoring those positions until you switch back, so they'd sit without stop-loss protection.

Tip: use the Test button on Settings → API Keys. It calls Trading 212 and shows the account balance and whether it's practice or real money — the quickest way to confirm your keys are correct and not swapped.

Getting set up

The bot needs four integrations, all entered on Settings → API Keys:

  • Finnhub — quotes, company news and profiles.
  • Tiingo — historical price candles for indicators.
  • Claude — news-sentiment scoring and written narratives.
  • Trading 212 — a key + secret pair for whichever mode(s) you'll run.

The approval flow

Approval is currently {{ approvalOn() ? 'ON' : 'OFF' }}. When on, the daily Report lists the proposed buys and nothing executes until you approve on the Trades → Approvals tab. There's a daily window (the Report tells you the cutoff); no action means no trades that day. When off, approved-by-default buy signals execute automatically at the Execution window.

Approvals are per-day and per-mode. Same-day buys that get re-triggered after a position closes are handled automatically without needing re-approval.

Readiness
A per-feature scorecard of how close the system is to being trusted with a given capability (e.g. live trading, refinement), based on your accumulated trade history.
Refinement
The monthly process that correlates each component's scores against your actual trade outcomes and proposes weight adjustments — statistically gated so it doesn't chase noise.
Setup type
The chart pattern behind a signal: oversold recovery, breakout, momentum continuation, volume spike, or trend following.
Circuit breaker
A safety flag raised if the portfolio falls by the daily-loss threshold in one day. It alerts — it does not auto-sell.
