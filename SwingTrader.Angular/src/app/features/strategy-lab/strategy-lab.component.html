Find optimum weights

The optimizer runs TWO search pools against the historic dataset. A deterministic sweep tries ~400 dial variations around your current production settings — single-dial nudges, pair shifts between every signal, Buy-threshold variants, structurally different mixes, bear-autopause toggles, and a dense random fill of the joint weights + threshold space. An ML-guided search then runs CMA-ES (a gradient-free evolutionary optimizer) from 30 independent starting points scattered across the same space, with successive halving: every start gets one cheap screening generation, the best half get a few more, and the final 5 get a deep, fully adapted search — broad where nothing is known yet, deep where early evidence says it's worth it. Which starts survive each round is decided by split-half consistency, not the headline number: each candidate is scored on the WORSE of its two train-window halves, so a config that only won by getting lucky in one stretch of the window gets cut early rather than spending deep-search budget on it, and the winner shown below still has to earn its keep separately on the held-out remainder it never saw. Together the two pools try ~1,200 candidates — with that many tried, out-of-sample validation matters more than ever, since the best of that many on one window is almost guaranteed to look good by chance even before the consistency screen. Both search pools only redistribute weight among the six components historic data exists for (RSI, MACD, Volume, Setup quality, Relative strength, Price level); the other two (News sentiment, Fundamental momentum) are held at your production values, because the backtest scores them neutral and "tuning" them would only produce conclusions that don't carry over to live trading. Candidates are judged on market-adjusted expectancy (each trade's return minus SPY's move over the same days), with guardrails: at least {{ 40 }} trades, and a max drawdown no worse than 1.25× the baseline's.
